A Red Flag

Objectives
  1. • Compare and contrast the morphologic features of apoptosis and necrosis.
  2. • List physiologic and pathologic examples of apoptosis.
  3. • Compare and contrast the extrinsic and intrinsic pathways of apoptosis.

A 55-year-old, healthy man was noted to have elevations in liver enzymes during his annual physical. After questioning, he stated that he used intravenous drugs sporadically while in college and currently drank socially. Physical exam was negative for jaundice, ascites, and edema. There was no hepatomegaly. A stool test for occult blood was negative.

Laboratory tests were significant for a positive anti-HCV antibody test. Subsequent HCV RNA quantitation showed >1 million IU/L. A liver biopsy was performed and showed scattered shrunken, deeply eosinophilic hepatocytes (arrow) and a lymphocytic infiltrate.


1
Which type of process is the specified cell undergoing?
Show answer

The cell is undergoing apoptosis mediated by cytotoxic T lymphocytes specific for hepatitis C antigens. Features indicative of apoptosis seen in this image include cell shrinking (in contrast to the cell swelling seen in necrosis) and the absence of a neutrophilic infiltration (also associated with necrosis).

2
What is apoptosis?
Show answer

Apoptosis is a pathway of cell death induced by a tightly regulated suicide program in which cells destined to die activate a cascade of enzymes that degrade the cells’ own nuclear DNA and nuclear and cytoplasmic proteins.


3
What are some physiologic causes of apoptosis?
Show answer

Examples of physiologic processes that occur via apoptosis include (1) the removal of cells during embryogenesis, an important part of the processes that lead to the development of many tissues and organs; (2) the involution of hormone-dependent tissues after hormone withdrawal, such as endometrial cell breakdown during the menstrual cycle; (3) the elimination of cells that fail certain developmental milestones, such as B and T cell progenitors that fail to undergo antigen receptor gene rearrangement successfully; (4) the deletion of potentially harmful self-reactive immature B and T cells; and (5) death of host cells that have served their useful purpose, such as neutrophils in an acute inflammatory response.

4
What are some pathologic causes of apoptosis?
Show answer

Examples of pathologic causes of apoptosis include DNA damage, accumulation of misfolded proteins, cell death in certain infections (e.g., viral hepatitis, as in this case) and atrophy following duct obstruction (e.g., in the pancreas, parotid gland, kidney).


5
What are the morphologic features of apoptosis?
Show answer

Cell shrinkage, chromatin condensation, formation of cytoplasmic blebs and apoptotic bodies, and phagocytosis of apoptotic cells or cell bodies.

6
What are caspases and what is their role in apoptosis?
Show answer

Caspases (so named because they are cysteine proteases that cleave proteins after aspartic residues) are enzymes that once activated start the process of apoptosis.

7
How are caspases activated?
Show answer

Two distinct pathways converge on the caspase cascade: the mitochondrial pathway and the death receptor pathway. Although these pathways can intersect, they are generally induced under different conditions, involve different molecules, and serve distinct roles in physiology and disease.
