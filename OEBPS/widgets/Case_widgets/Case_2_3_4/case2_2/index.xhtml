Leukocytes Lose Their Way

Normal Reference Range Table
Objectives
  1. • Compare and contrast the defects in innate immunity caused by deficiencies in leukocyte function and in the complement system.
  2. • Describe the pathway of leukocyte migration from the blood into tissue sites of infection and necrosis.
  3. • Describe the functions of the major adhesion molecules involved in this process.

A 4-year-old boy presented to an urgent care clinic for evaluation of recurrent acute otitis media. In addition to three prior episodes of acute otitis media requiring multiple courses of antibiotics, his medical history was notable for delayed separation of the umbilical cord at day 60 (normal 3–45 days), recurrent gingivitis, and multiple bouts of streptococcal pharyngitis. In addition, his parents reported that his superficial scratches were slow to heal. In view of his recurrent infections and poor wound healing, he was referred to the pediatric immunology clinic. Laboratory analysis revealed an elevated white blood cell count of 34.8 (normal 4.5–11K/mm3) and 65% neutrophils (normal 54%–62%). Serum IgG, IgA, and IgM were elevated.

1
What are some of the possible causes of multiple bacterial infections in a child?
Show answer

Inherited defects in innate immunity predispose children to recurrent bacterial infections. The majority of these defects affect leukocyte function or the complement system. Defects in leukocyte function include defects in leukocyte adhesion, phagolysosome function, microbicidal activity and Toll-like receptor (TLR) signaling. Hereditary deficiencies have been described for virtually all components of the complement system and for several of its regulators. Defects in adaptive immunity, such as X-linked agammaglobulinemia and other disorders, also lead to increased susceptibility to infection.

Further testing revealed very low levels of β2 integrin expression on blood cells (2% of normal). Genetic analysis revealed a mutation in the ITGB2 gene that encodes β2 integrin.

2
What is the function of β2 integrin?
Show answer

Integrins are heterodimers composed of alpha and beta subunits that interact noncovalently. CD18 is another name for the β2 integrin subunit that is a component of the integrins LFA-1 and MAC-1. In the absence of β2 integrin, neither LFA-1 nor MAC-1 is produced.

3
What is the role of integrins in the immune response?
Show answer

Leukocytes typically circulate in the blood, allowing them to reach sites of infection and tissue damage anywhere in the body. Leukocyte migration from the blood into tissues is divided into steps, all of which are in response to the activation of endothelial cells and leukocytes by cytokines and other mediators of inflammation. Initially, leukocytes move towards the vessel walls, a process called margination. Subsequently, selectins mediate weak attachments to the endothelium causing the leukocytes to roll along the endothelial surface. Next, integrins enable the leukocytes to bind firmly to the endothelium. Finally, the leukocytes migrate through inter-endothelial spaces into the extravascular space.



4
Why was this patient susceptible to recurrent infections?
Show answer

This patient has leukocyte adhesion deficiency 1 (LAD-1). He has essentially no β2 integrin (CD18) and therefore lacks both LFA-1 and MAC-1. LFA-1 is usually present on neutrophils, T cells, and some monocytes, while MAC-1 is found on monocytes and dendritic cells. The patient’s frequent infections are due to the absence of LFA-1; neutrophils are the first responders to bacterial infections. Without LFA-1, they are unable to bind stably to the endothelium and therefore cannot extravasate into the extravascular space.

5
Why is this patient’s WBC count so high?
Show answer

This patient most likely has multiple sites of infection throughout his body. These infections stimulate the inflammatory response. TNF and IL-1 stimulate accelerated release of cells from the bone marrow, and CSFs stimulate production of new leukocytes. However, the neutrophils cannot leave the bloodstream, and this, combined with the increased production of neutrophils, elevates the WBC count.

6
Why were the serum IgM and IgG levels elevated?
Show answer

The likely explanation is that the adaptive immune system is intact, therefore the patient can make antibodies. The widespread infection has stimulated stronger than usual immune responses.
