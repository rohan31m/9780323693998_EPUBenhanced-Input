A (Non)illuminating Case

Normal Reference Range Table
Objectives
  1. • Compare and contrast the morphologic and clinical features of seminomatous and nonseminomatous germ cell tumors.
  2. • Describe the use of the serum markers human chorionic gonadotropin (HCG) and ɑ-fetoprotein (AFP) in the diagnosis and treatment of germ cell tumors.

A 45-year-old previously healthy man with a past history of cryptorchidism that was repaired at age 5 presented with painless enlargement of the left testis. The mass was opaque on transillumination.

1
What is the significance of the absence of transillumination?
Show answer

Transillumination can be seen in cases of hydrocele, the most common cause of scrotal swelling. Hydrocele is caused by an accumulation of serous fluid within the tunica vaginalis. It may be idiopathic or arise in response to neighboring infections or tumors. The clear fluid of a hydrocele allows light to pass through (transluminescence), distinguishing it from solid tumors and collections of blood, pus, or lymph, all of which are cloudy or opaque.

2
What is the clinical significance of the patient’s history of cryptorchidism?
Show answer

Cryptorchidism (the failure of descent of the testis into the scrotum) is associated with a 3- to 5-fold increased risk for testicular cancer. Orchiopexy reduces the risk, but not to baseline.

A testicular ultrasound examination revealed the enlargement to be composed of soft tissue without a cystic component.

3
Which laboratory tests should be ordered?
Show answer

In the setting of a solid mass in the testis, a testicular neoplasm is high on the differential diagnosis. 95% of testicular tumors are of germ cell origin and are subclassified into seminomas and nonseminomatous tumors. The latter includes embryonal carcinoma, yolk sac tumor, choriocarcinoma, spermatocytic tumor, and mixed tumors. α-Fetoprotein (AFP) is elevated in a proportion of yolk sac tumors and mixed tumors, and human chorionic gonadotrophin (HCG) is elevated in a subset of seminomas, choriocarcinomas, and mixed tumors. Therefore, serum levels of AFP and HCG should be assessed.

Laboratory data included serum HCG of 90 mU/mL (reference range 5 mU/mL) and AFP of 7 ng/mL (reference range 0–44 ng/mL). An abdominal CT scan revealed para-aortic lymphadenopathy, with the largest lymph node being about 2cm in size; a chest x-ray was normal. A radical left orchiectomy was scheduled.

4
Why was the orchiectomy scheduled without a biopsy?
Show answer

Although most major surgeries require a tissue diagnosis, testicular masses are not biopsied, particularly in the setting of elevated HCG or AFP, since the likelihood is very high that it is a malignant tumor. A biopsy risks spreading tumor cells outside of the testis and preventing surgical cure.

A radical left orchiectomy and lymph node biopsy were performed. The left testicle was notable for a 4-cm, tan nodule with adjacent normal testicular parenchyma. The black areas are due to ink painted on the specimen in order to allow evaluation of the surgical margins.


  • Testicle, gross
  • Tumor
  • Normal testicle
  • Inked surface
5
What is the most likely diagnosis?
Show answer

In view of the mildly elevated HCG, the normal AFP level, and the homogeneous cut surface of the tumor, this is most likely a seminoma. Nonseminomatous germ cell tumors typically have a heterogeneous appearance with areas of necrosis, cyst formation, and hemorrhage, as in the embryonal carcinoma depicted below.

  • Embryonal carcinoma, gross
  • Hemorrhagic area
6
What are the histologic features of seminoma?
Show answer

Seminomas are composed of sheets of uniform polygonal cells divided into ill-defined nodules by fibrous trabeculae. The cells have clear cytoplasm, round nuclei, and prominent nucleoli. A lymphocytic infiltrate of activated T cells is common. These T cells are often found in association with seminomas, possibly because of the expression of antigens in malignant germ cells that are recognized as foreign by the host immune system.

  • Seminoma, histology
  • Seminoma cells
  • Fibrous trabecula
  • Mitotic figure
  • Lymphocytes
7
What is the most common acquired cytogenetic abnormality in germ cell tumors?
Show answer

A high fraction of germ cell tumors are associated with isochromosome 12q, an abnormality that is quite specific for this tumor type in both males and females, in whom the tumor is known as dysgerminoma rather than seminoma.

Histologic examination of the lymph node biopsy confirmed metastatic seminoma.

8
What is the prognosis for seminoma?
Show answer

More than 95% of patients with tumors confined to the testicle or metastatic to the subdiaphragmatic lymph nodes are cured.

9
What is the appropriate therapy to treat the patient’s lymph node metastases?
Show answer

In patients with minimal lymphadenopathy (largest node 2cm or less) the primary mode of therapy is radiotherapy. More extensive or bulky lymph node disease is an indication for treatment with chemotherapy. Fortunately, seminoma is very sensitive to both radiation therapy and platinum-based chemotherapy and is therefore highly curable.

10
Why did this patient have increased serum HCG?
Show answer

Approximately 10% to 15% of seminomas contain syncytial giant cells that elaborate HCG. In these patients, serum HCG is moderately elevated. This feature does not affect treatment or prognosis. These syncytial giant cells are also the source of the increased serum HCG in testicular choriocarcinomas, a highly malignant form of testicular tumor.

11
How do choriocarcinomas typically present?
Show answer

Choriocarcinomas often cause no testicular enlargement and are detected only as a small palpable nodule. In its “pure” form, choriocarcinoma is rare, constituting less than 1% of all germ cell tumors. Choriocarcinoma spreads predominantly and rapidly by the bloodstream. Therefore, lungs and liver are involved early in virtually every case.

12
What are the histologic features of choriocarcinoma?
Show answer

Histologically, the tumors contain two cell types: syncytiotrophoblasts and cytotrophoblasts. Syncytiotrophoblasts are large multinucleated cells with abundant eosinophilic vacuolated cytoplasm containing HCG, which is readily detected by immunohistochemistry. Cytotrophoblasts are more regular and tend to be polygonal with distinct borders and clear cytoplasm. Syncytiotrophoblasts may be seen in any germ cell tumor; however, a diagnosis of choriocarcinoma requires syncytiotrophoblasts and cytotrophoblasts. This neoplasm can also arise in the female genital tract.

  • Choriocarcinoma, histology
  • Syncytiotrophoblast
  • Cytotrophoblast
13
What is another testicular tumor that often has elevated HCG?
Show answer

Mixed tumors may have elevated levels of both HCG and AFP. The most common components of a mixed tumor are embryonal carcinoma, teratoma, and yolk sac tumor.

14
What is the typical clinical presentation of yolk sac tumor?
Show answer

Yolk sac tumor is the most common primary testicular neoplasm in children younger than 3 years of age; in this age group, it has a very good prognosis. In adults, yolk sac tumors are often admixed with embryonal carcinoma. The tumors are large and may be well demarcated. Ninety percent of patients have elevated AFP.

15
Which other tumor frequently expresses AFP?
Show answer

The only other tumor that expresses AFP (other than mixed tumors with a yolk sac component) is hepatocellular carcinoma.

16
What are the histologic features of yolk sac tumor?
Show answer

Yolk sac tumors are composed of low cuboidal to columnar epithelial cells that form microcysts, lacelike (reticular) patterns, sheets, glands, and papillae. A distinctive feature is the presence of structures resembling primitive glomeruli, the so-called Schiller-Duval bodies.

  • Yolk sac tumor, histology
  • Schiller-Duvall bodies
  • Microcysts
17
How does embryonal carcinoma differ from yolk sac tumor?
Show answer

Embryonal carcinoma is most common in men aged 20 to 30. The tumors may be small, even in patients with systemic metastases. It does not express AFP, although most tumors are admixed with other germ cell tumors, including yolk sac tumor (which does express AFP). Pure embryonal carcinomas account for only 2%–3% of all testicular germ cell tumors. Histologically, the tumor cells are large and have basophilic cytoplasm, large nuclei, and prominent nucleoli. The neoplastic cells may be arranged in solid sheets or form primitive glandular structures.

  • Embryonal carcinoma
  • Solid areas
  • Gland-like structures
